## The Challenge

Nova Financial had outgrown its original identity. The wordmark was a Series-Seed compromise, the marketing site was a Webflow build that took 4.1 seconds to render the hero on a mid-tier phone, and the brand was actively losing the room in enterprise sales calls. The team had 14 weeks of runway before a planned Series A and a list of unresolved diligence items that started with "the website".

The CEO was direct on our first call: the brand had to feel like a regulated fintech the day it shipped, not three months after. The site had to load fast enough to survive a CMO's mobile signal in the back of an Uber. And the new identity had to give a 22-person team the visual cover to act like a 100-person one.

## Our Approach

We compressed the rebrand into a five-week sprint with three workstreams running in parallel: identity, web platform, and a tightened message architecture. We embedded with Nova's leadership four days a week and locked scope on day three.

Instead of running a traditional discovery, we ran ten customer calls in the first eight days — five existing Nova customers, five lost-deal interviews. The pattern was clear: prospects trusted the product the moment they used it, but they did not trust the surface long enough to get there. Every design and engineering decision after that pointed at one number: time-to-trust.

We rebuilt the brand around a single, restrained mark and a typographic system that read as institutional without feeling stiff. We wrote a banned-words list on day five and held every page of copy against it. We picked Next.js 15 on Vercel for the web platform, with a typed CMS layer so Nova's marketing team could ship updates without a developer in the loop.

## What We Shipped

- A new wordmark, logotype, and full type system in editable vector with light and dark variants.
- A messaging architecture and homepage built around four customer-validated proof points, replacing the eleven-section legacy site.
- A production Next.js 15 marketing site on Vercel with typed content, MDX case studies, and a programmatic SEO layer covering 142 long-tail finance queries.
- A component library mapped 1:1 from Figma to the codebase, so future pages take hours, not weeks.
- A redesigned product onboarding flow that moved the "aha" moment from screen seven to screen two.
- Analytics, error tracking, and a weekly performance budget wired into CI so regressions never reach production.

## The Outcome

The new site went live on a Tuesday. By Friday, Largest Contentful Paint had settled at 1.2 seconds on the median mobile session, down from 4.1 seconds on the legacy build. Paid acquisition CAC dropped 28 percent within the first thirty days as the same campaigns landed on a faster, clearer page.

Six months in, organic traffic was up 312 percent against the prior period, driven by the programmatic SEO layer and a content cadence Nova's two-person marketing team can sustain without burning out. The new identity carried Nova through a $24 million Series A eleven weeks after relaunch, with the lead investor citing the rebrand on the term-sheet call. Nova extended the engagement into a six-month retainer and we are now their on-call design and engineering team while their first in-house designer ramps up.
